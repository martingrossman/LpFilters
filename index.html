Algorithm quick guide
Ground truth: ideal reference signal before measurement noise.
Raw: noisy measurements with no smoothing or model assumptions.
Rolling average: moving mean over a window; simple smoothing but adds lag.
Rolling regression: local line fit in a window; output is fitted value and slope.
α-β filter: constant-velocity tracker; α corrects position, β corrects velocity.
α-β-γ filter: constant-acceleration tracker; adds γ to adapt acceleration changes.
Regression slope is estimated directly from a local linear fit. Average gradient is derived from the smoothed output, so it often lags more.
